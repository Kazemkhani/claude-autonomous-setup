5 contrarian stances
Amir H. Kazemkhani · 2026
01
Slow down on features. Speed up on architecture.
Shipping the wrong abstraction in AI is 10× harder to undo than in a normal SaaS. The state machine you choose on day 3 is the one you debug in month 6.
02
Don't demo things you haven't run in production.
AI demos lie. The failure modes only appear under real load, real data, real users who say things your test cases didn't cover.
03
Fewer agents. More observability.
Every agent you add is a surface that can fail silently. A 3-agent system you can debug beats a 9-agent system you can't.
04
Log everything, even the calls that succeed.
Success logs tell you what good looks like. Without them, you can't tell if a regression broke a previously-working path.
05
Ship slow. Learn fast. The cadence is not the same thing.
Deliberate shipping + fast feedback loops beats "move fast" every time for AI systems that handle real money or real customers.
